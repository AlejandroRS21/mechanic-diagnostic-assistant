=== BRAKE PAD REPLACEMENT ===
Repair: Brake Pad Replacement

Required Tools:
- Jack and jack stands
- Lug wrench
- C-clamp or brake caliper tool
- Socket set
- Torque wrench
- Wire brush

Required Parts:
- New brake pads (front or rear set)
- Brake cleaner
- Anti-seize compound
- Optional: New brake hardware kit

Safety Precautions:
⚠️ NEVER work under a car supported only by a jack
⚠️ Always use jack stands
⚠️ Wear safety glasses
⚠️ Wash hands after working with brake dust (may contain asbestos)

Estimated Time: 1-2 hours per axle
Difficulty Level: Intermediate

Step-by-Step Procedure:

1. Preparation
   - Park on level ground and engage parking brake
   - Loosen lug nuts while vehicle is on ground (do not remove)
   - Lift vehicle with jack and secure on jack stands
   - Remove wheels

2. Remove Old Brake Pads
   - Locate brake caliper
   - Remove caliper bolts (usually 2) - DO NOT disconnect brake line
   - Lift caliper off rotor and suspend with wire (don't let it hang by brake hose)
   - Remove old brake pads from caliper bracket
   - Remove caliper bracket if replacing rotors (2 larger bolts)

3. Inspect Components
   - Check brake rotors for scoring, cracks, or excessive wear
   - Measure rotor thickness with micrometer (compare to minimum spec)
   - Inspect caliper slides/pins for corrosion or binding
   - Check brake hoses for cracks or damage

4. Prepare for Installation
   - Use C-clamp to compress caliper piston slowly back into bore
   - Clean caliper bracket and slides with wire brush
   - Apply brake lubricant to caliper slides (NOT the pads themselves)
   - Clean rotor surface with brake cleaner

5. Install New Brake Pads
   - Install new brake hardware clips if included
   - Place new brake pads in caliper bracket (inner and outer)
   - Reinstall caliper bracket if removed (torque to spec: typically 70-90 ft-lbs)
   - Lower caliper over new pads
   - Install and torque caliper bolts (typically 25-35 ft-lbs)

6. Repeat for Other Side
   - Complete same procedure on opposite wheel

7. Finishing Steps
   - Reinstall wheels and hand-tighten lug nuts
   - Lower vehicle to ground
   - Torque lug nuts in star pattern to manufacturer spec (typically 80-100 ft-lbs)
   - Pump brake pedal several times until firm before driving
   - Check brake fluid level in reservoir (should be at MAX line)

8. Break-In Period (IMPORTANT)
   - Drive at moderate speeds for first 200 miles
   - Avoid hard braking initially to allow pads to seat properly
   - First 30 stops: brake from 30 mph to 5 mph with moderate pressure
   - Allow brakes to cool between stops

9. Final Checks
   - After test drive, recheck lug nut torque
   - Listen for abnormal brake noises
   - Verify brake pedal feels firm and responsive


=== CATALYTIC CONVERTER REPLACEMENT ===
Repair: Catalytic Converter Replacement

Required Tools:
- Jack and jack stands
- Penetrating oil (PB Blaster, WD-40)
- Oxygen sensor socket
- Socket set and ratchet
- Reciprocating saw or cutting wheel (for rusted bolts)
- Wire brush
- Torque wrench

Required Parts:
- New catalytic converter
- Exhaust gaskets
- Exhaust hangers (if damaged)
- Anti-seize compound
- New bolts/nuts (often needed due to rust)

Safety Precautions:
⚠️ Allow exhaust system to cool completely (at least 1 hour after driving)
⚠️ Wear safety glasses and gloves
⚠️ Work in well-ventilated area
⚠️ Use proper lifting equipment

Estimated Time: 2-4 hours
Difficulty Level: Intermediate to Advanced

Step-by-Step Procedure:

1. Preparation
   - Let exhaust cool completely
   - Spray all exhaust bolts with penetrating oil
   - Wait 15-30 minutes for penetrant to work
   - Lift vehicle and secure on jack stands

2. Disconnect Oxygen Sensors
   - Locate oxygen sensors before and after catalytic converter
   - Disconnect electrical connectors
   - Remove sensors with oxygen sensor socket
   - Store sensors safely (they're reusable if not damaged)

3. Remove Catalytic Converter
   - Support exhaust system with jack or hanger
   - Remove bolts connecting cat to exhaust manifold/header
   - Remove bolts connecting cat to mid-pipe
   - If bolts are seized: cut with reciprocating saw (may need to cut pipe)
   - Lower old catalytic converter

4. Prepare for Installation
   - Clean mounting surfaces with wire brush
   - Remove old gasket material
   - Check exhaust hangers and replace if damaged
   - Apply anti-seize to new bolt threads

5. Install New Catalytic Converter
   - Position new cat with new gaskets
   - Start all bolts hand-tight first
   - Align exhaust system properly (check clearance from chassis)
   - Torque bolts to specification (typically 25-35 ft-lbs)

6. Reinstall Oxygen Sensors
   - Apply anti-seize to sensor threads (avoid getting on sensor tip)
   - Install sensors and torque (typically 30-35 ft-lbs)
   - Reconnect electrical connectors

7. Final Steps
   - Lower vehicle
   - Start engine and check for exhaust leaks
   - Clear diagnostic codes with OBD-II scanner
   - Test drive and verify check engine light stays off


=== SPARK PLUG REPLACEMENT ===
Repair: Spark Plug Replacement

Required Tools:
- Spark plug socket (5/8" or 13/16" depending on vehicle)
- Socket extension
- Ratchet
- Torque wrench
- Spark plug gap tool
- Dielectric grease
- Compressed air (optional but recommended)

Required Parts:
- New spark plugs (correct type for your engine)
- Anti-seize compound (for aluminum heads)

Safety Precautions:
⚠️ Work on cool engine
⚠️ Disconnect battery negative terminal
⚠️ Keep workspace clean to prevent debris entering cylinders

Estimated Time: 30 minutes - 2 hours (depending on engine accessibility)
Difficulty Level: Beginner to Intermediate

Step-by-Step Procedure:

1. Preparation
   - Let engine cool completely
   - Disconnect battery negative terminal
   - Locate all spark plugs
   - Label spark plug wires if not using coil-on-plug system

2. Access Spark Plugs
   - Remove engine cover if present
   - On coil-on-plug systems: disconnect electrical connector from ignition coil
   - Remove ignition coil or spark plug wire (one at a time)
   - Use compressed air to blow debris from around spark plug

3. Remove Old Spark Plugs
   - Insert spark plug socket and extension
   - Turn counterclockwise
   - If resistance felt, apply penetrating oil and wait
   - Remove spark plug completely
   - Inspect old plug for fouling, wear, or damage

4. Gap New Spark Plugs
   - Check spark plug gap with gap tool
   - Compare to vehicle specification (usually 0.028" - 0.060")
   - Adjust if necessary by carefully bending ground electrode

5. Install New Spark Plugs
   - Apply small amount of anti-seize to threads (aluminum heads only)
   - Start spark plug by hand to avoid cross-threading
   - Tighten with socket:
     * Gasket seat plugs: 1/4 turn after gasket contacts head
     * Tapered seat plugs: 1/16 turn after seat contacts
   - Or torque to specification (typically 15-20 ft-lbs for 14mm)

6. Reinstall Ignition Components
   - Apply dielectric grease inside spark plug boot
   - Reinstall ignition coil or spark plug wire
   - Reconnect electrical connector
   - Ensure secure connection

7. Repeat for All Cylinders
   - Work on one cylinder at a time to avoid mixing up wires

8. Final Steps
   - Reconnect battery
   - Start engine and listen for smooth operation
   - Check for misfires or rough idle


=== OIL CHANGE PROCEDURE ===
Repair: Engine Oil and Filter Change

Required Tools:
- Oil filter wrench
- Socket set or wrench for drain plug
- Oil drain pan
- Funnel
- Jack and jack stands (if needed)
- Shop towels

Required Parts:
- Engine oil (correct weight and quantity for your vehicle)
- Oil filter (correct part number)
- Drain plug washer/gasket (if reusable type is worn)

Safety Precautions:
⚠️ Avoid hot oil - can cause severe burns
⚠️ Dispose of used oil properly at recycling center
⚠️ Wear gloves and safety glasses

Estimated Time: 30-45 minutes
Difficulty Level: Beginner

Step-by-Step Procedure:

1. Warm Up Engine
   - Run engine for 5 minutes to warm oil (flows better)
   - Turn off engine and wait 2-3 minutes
   - Lift vehicle if necessary and secure on jack stands

2. Drain Old Oil
   - Locate oil drain plug under engine
   - Position drain pan underneath
   - Remove drain plug (counterclockwise)
   - Allow oil to drain completely (5-10 minutes)

3. Replace Oil Filter
   - Locate oil filter
   - Use filter wrench to remove old filter (counterclockwise)
   - Clean filter mounting surface on engine
   - Apply thin coat of new oil to rubber gasket on new filter
   - Install new filter hand-tight only (do NOT use wrench)

4. Reinstall Drain Plug
   - Replace drain plug washer if needed
   - Install drain plug and tighten (typically 25-30 ft-lbs)
   - Wipe area clean

5. Add New Oil
   - Lower vehicle if raised
   - Remove oil filler cap
   - Use funnel to add correct amount of oil
   - Check owner's manual for capacity
   - Install oil filler cap

6. Check Oil Level
   - Start engine and run for 1 minute
   - Turn off and wait 2 minutes
   - Remove dipstick, wipe clean, reinsert fully
   - Remove again and check level (should be between MIN and MAX)
   - Add oil if needed

7. Final Checks
   - Inspect under vehicle for leaks
   - Reset oil life indicator if equipped
   - Record mileage and date for next change


These repair guides provide general procedures. Always consult your vehicle's service manual for specific torque specifications, procedures, and safety precautions for your particular make and model.
